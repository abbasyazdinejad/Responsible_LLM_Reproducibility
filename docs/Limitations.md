# Limitations

- **Three predefined personas.** Province, sex, and age vary jointly across only three personas, so these
  attributes are confounded and any subgroup pattern is exploratory, not a demonstrated disparity. The
  personas are not statistically representative.
- **Single-rater pilot.** The human rubric pilot has one rater and usable variable ratings for 13 DeepSeek
  responses only; inter-rater reliability could not be computed, and no model comparison is drawn from it.
- **Lexical markers.** Safety/cultural markers and FNMWCF coverage are keyword-based proxies; keyword
  presence does not establish contextual appropriateness or cultural adequacy.
- **Readability ≠ comprehension.** Flesch–Kincaid estimates textual complexity, not patient comprehension.
- **Trimming effect.** The 180-word display trim materially reduces the measured DeepSeek crisis marker;
  content markers are therefore reported on full responses and the trimming effect is reported explicitly.
- **No Indigenous-identity variable.** The CCHS PUMF contains no direct Indigenous-identity variable; the
  prompts are FNMWCF-informed, not community-validated.
- **English only.** All prompts and responses are in English; no multilingual evaluation was performed.
- **Mutable model tags.** Immutable checkpoint digests were not recorded; exact re-generation is not
  guaranteed, so the stored responses are provided.
- **No clinical outcomes.** The study measures model outputs, not clinical outcomes; it cannot establish
  real-world harm.
